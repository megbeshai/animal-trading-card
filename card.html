Orangutan

Orangutans sometimes like to blow raspberries.

  • Scientific Name:
    Pongo
  • Habitat:
    Rainforests of Borneo and Sumatra
  • Daily Activities:
    Orangutans build nests for daytime and nighttime use. Baby Orangutans leave their mothers to practice nesting at the age of six months and have mastered this survival skill by the time they turn three years old.
  • Ape Smarts:
    Orangutans are incredibly intelligent and capable of using a computer to play games, solving invisible displacement problems, and engaging in calculated reciprocity.

Orangutans are considered to be one of the Great Apes. They have thick stocky bodies, long arms, and are covered in reddish-brown hair. The name orangutan comes from the Malay and Indonesian words meaning forest person. Like people, orangutans have four fingers and an opposable thumb on each hand. Unlike humans, they also have opposable big toes. Male orangutans have pronounced cheek flaps.
